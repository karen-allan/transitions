Our Modern World - Are We Really Healthy?

Modern science and medicine inventions and innovations have been invaluable in providing us the ability to live longer lives. But are we actually healthier? Even more so, are we actually happier?

In 1920–1922 life expectancy in Canada for men in was 58.8 years and life expectancy for women was 60.6 years. In 2018, life expectancy for men in Canada was 80.9 years and the life expectancy for women was 84.7 years.

In past times, people died of acute illnesses such as flus, viruses and from accidents. Increases in safety such as seat belts for cars and vaccines for viruses, emergency room interventions, antibiotics etc. have been effective to the point where now only 30% of the population (CHPCA Fact Sheet – Hospice Palliative Care in Canada 2014) dies from an acute illness or a sudden accident.

This means, that 70% of us will eventually contract a chronic illness. People who have chronic issues are often left to fend on their own, without a definite plan for gaining back their health. They may go from doctor, to chiropractor, to internist to surgeon and in a worst case scenario, end up addicted to pain medications. They often hear the dreaded words "there is nothing more we can do". In all these cases the patient is handing over most of the responsibility for their health and well-being from - an all too often - ten minute consult".

The initial purpose of a hospital was to give help to those in immediate need due to accidents or acute illnesses. It was never meant to be the place we go if we are suffering from a chronic illness, let alone when we are dying. But the very sad fact is that most people, 70% of us, will die in a hospital (CHPCA Fact Sheet – Hospice Palliative Care in Canada Updated October 2015) often very alone, confused and scared.
