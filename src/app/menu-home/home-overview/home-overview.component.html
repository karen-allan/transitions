Facing the Facts

In 1920–1922 life expectancy in Canada for men in was 58.8 years. In 1920–1922 life expectancy for women was 60.6 years. In 2018, life expectancy for men in Canada was 80.9 years. In 2018,life expectancy for women was 84.7 years. In the past people died of acute illnesses and from accidents. Now with modern medicine only 30% of the population dies this way. This means, that 70% of us will eventually contract a chronic illness. People who have chronic issues are often left to fend on their own. Palliative care is under-funded and families must take on patient care. Although most of us want to die at home, 70% will die in a hospital.
